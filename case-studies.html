Healthcare

Saint Marguerite Regional Medical Center

Riverdale, IL · 620 beds · 1.4M sq ft

An 18-month BMS migration with zero ICU downtime.

Saint Marguerite's controls platform had been three generations old, with critical care areas riding a pneumatic loop the manufacturer had stopped supporting in 2011. Replacing it without disrupting OR or ICU service was the constraint that shaped every decision.

We phased the migration room-by-room over eighteen months, running the new BACnet/IP network in parallel with the legacy pneumatic loop for each suite, switching only after seventy-two hours of side-by-side validation. The ICU and four ORs never came offline.

Linden Park, NY · 1908 Beaux-Arts · 220k sq ft

Linden Avenue Museum of Art — humidity envelope ±3% RH.

The Linden's textile collection requires a tighter humidity envelope than most clean rooms. The 2019 mechanical retrofit had delivered the equipment but not the intelligence — operators relied on a phone tree for envelope deviations.

We installed an HBMS layer over the existing BMS, mapping every gallery to its collection profile and writing collection-specific deviation rules. The night-shift response time fell from ninety minutes to under two.

Higher Ed

Riverbend University Sciences Quad

Riverbend, ON · 4 buildings · shared central plant

31% kW/ton reduction after a sequence rework.

Four research buildings on a shared chilled-water loop, none of which trusted the others' setpoints. Each had quietly overridden the central plant's reset schedule to guarantee its own supply temperature, locking the chillers into the worst case for the whole loop.

The fix was almost entirely software. We rewrote the central-plant reset logic to honor the most-demanding building's load while protecting the rest with floating minimum-flow controls, and we built a dashboard so the four building operators could finally see what the others were asking for.

Hollis & Park HQ Tower · Chicago, IL · 22 stories · 480k sq ft

Post-return-to-office IAQ overhaul.

After two years of intermittent occupancy, Hollis & Park returned to a five-day office model — and discovered that their ventilation schedule, sized for 2019 occupancy patterns, was simultaneously over-ventilating empty floors and under-ventilating dense conference rooms.

We deployed CO₂-driven demand-controlled ventilation on every floor, tied to the existing rooftop air handlers and supplemented by per-floor humidity and PM2.5 sensing. Tenants now receive a quarterly IAQ report; the operations team gets a daily one.
