Insights / Physics

PSYCHROMETRICS
FOR OPERATORS.

HVAC is just psychrometrics, applied at scale. Get four numbers and you can read any air-side problem.

Air is a mixture of dry air and water vapour, and almost every HVAC decision is really a question about that mixture. You don't need the whole chart in your head — you need four numbers and how they move.

The four numbers

Dry-bulb is the temperature you feel on a thermometer. Wet-bulb is what you'd read with a wet sock over the bulb — it folds in how much the air can still absorb. Dew point is where vapour starts to condense. Enthalpy is the total heat, sensible plus latent, and it's the one that decides whether your economiser should open.

Cooling coils don't chase temperature. They chase enthalpy.

Why operators care

A gallery stuck at 53% RH, a coil sweating, an economiser that opens at the wrong time — all of these are points on a psychrometric chart drifting where you don't want them. Plot the state, and the fix is usually obvious.
