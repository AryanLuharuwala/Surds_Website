Services

Four practices, one objective.

Specification, integration, analytics, and operation. Each one stands alone; together they are the difference between a building that works on day one and a building that still works on day one thousand.

01 · Design & specification

The contract documents that will be read in court.

We write the BMS portion of the contract documents — control narratives, sequences of operation, point lists, panel schedules, and submittal requirements — to a standard that holds up through three rounds of value engineering and survives the controls contractor's interpretation.

Typical engagement: 4–10% of mechanical fee, depending on building type and the level of integration with adjacent systems (security, lighting, metering, fire).

  • Owner project requirements (OPR) translation
  • Control narrative authorship in plain English, with diagrams
  • Point list (operational / diagnostic / archival, separated)
  • Open-protocol enforcement (BACnet, Modbus, MQTT)
  • Cyber requirements in the spec, not as a bolt-on
02 · Integration & commissioning

BACnet, Modbus, LonWorks, MQTT — pick three.

We integrate multi-vendor controls into one coherent system, then prove it works before the punch list is closed. Our commissioning includes functional performance testing of every sequence, not just every device — because devices pass tests every day inside buildings that don't.

We've handled phased migrations on operating hospitals, museums with humidity envelopes measured in single-digit percentages, and data centers that cannot lose a single CRAH unit during cutover. The first phase of every project is a network and protocol audit.

  • Protocol gateway design and configuration
  • Point mapping with Project Haystack or Brick schema
  • Functional performance testing, by sequence
  • Live cutover playbooks for occupied buildings
03 · Fault detection & analytics

Continuous commissioning, not annual audits.

A building's tune-up shouldn't be a once-a-year event. We deploy rule-based and ML-driven fault detection that runs every minute against your live data, flagging drifts, anomalies, and inefficiencies in priority order — and routing them into whatever ticketing or CMMS system your team already uses.

Our analytics layer is vendor-neutral. We work with SkySpark, Switch Automation, KGS Buildings, and bespoke pipelines built on InfluxDB/Grafana — whatever fits the owner's existing stack and operational team.

  • Rule libraries tuned to your building, not a generic template
  • Predictive maintenance for fans, pumps, and chillers
  • Energy attribution by equipment, zone, and use
  • Custom dashboards built around the operator's workflow

04 · Operations & tuning

We stay on the building.

The most consistent finding from our analytics is that buildings drift. Sequences are overridden during a heatwave and never restored; setpoints are widened to silence a complaint and forgotten; meters fail and nobody notices because the spreadsheet they fed is no longer being read.

Our operations engagement is a relationship, not a service contract. Monthly retunes, quarterly business reviews, and a named engineer who picks up the phone.

  • Monthly setpoint and sequence reviews
  • Seasonal optimization (summer/winter changeovers)
  • Retro-commissioning every 3–5 years
  • After-hours response, with a 30-minute SLA
